Clay Chronicles: A Beginner's Journey to Ceramics

Ceramics. Upon first glance, it may signify nothing more than a simple pastime - but it's more. It's an art, it's a skill, it's a way of life. Venturing on this journey is one that takes stamina, fortitude, and dertermination. But these sacrifices are worth it, for every morsel of hard work you put into honing the craft, you will find tenfold the amount of satisfaction. With that etablished, let us delve into my molding moments, my clayful beginnings, a glimpse into a ceramic beginner's diary. I hope you are as fired up to hear about my kilnside reflections as I am to convey these earthen explorations to you.

The First Step

A journey of a thousand miles begins with one step, and the first step to beginning one's relationship with ceramics begins with the careful curation of a pinch pot. The ideal position for a novice to begin, the pinch pot allows an aspiring ceramicist to get a feel for the clay.

Making My First Pinch Pot

Upon wedging my first block of clay, I was struck with a dire revalation. The legnth of my fingernails proved to be a hinderance to my ability to manipulate the reddish-brown, recycled clay! Enduring the struggles of moist, grainy, cakey clay under my nails, I braved the first day of pinch-pot curating. I cut my nails immediatley upon arriving home.

The Step Between the First and the Second

Once I had shortened the duration of my finger nails, I honed the intricate craft of pinch-pot curation. With this newly acquire power, I implemented these skills to devise a pumpkin of perfection - two pinch pots blended into a symmetrical sphere, etched with long indents that bore down the pumkin's side, and an unruly, charming stem - borne of pressing clay through a garlic press - that sprouted from the pumkin's head.
